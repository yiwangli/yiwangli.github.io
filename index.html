About me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate (on the job market!) in Education Policy Analysis and Leadership at the University of California Riverside, advised by Dr. Cassandra Guarino. My dissertation is on predicting college readiness using machine learning.

I work on understanding and applying machine learning and quantitative research methods in education. My research focuses on three areas: 1) using administrative data and predictive modeling to better assess risk and recommend early intervention, 2) evaluating the effects of policy changes on students, and 3) understanding the relationships between student characteristics and outcomes.

I am funded by the Dean's distinguished fellowship and multiple major research grants (NSF, IES, and Haynes).

I got my MS in Research, Evaluation, Measurement, and Statistics from UC Riverside in 2021, and a BS in Education and Public Policy (Sociology minor) from Penn State in 2016. Apart from research, I do wildlife photography and snowboarding. My wife and I have two lovely girls (4 and 1 yo). We lead a Chinese student fellowship at CBU (email me if you want to know about what we do!)
