About me

I’m a Ph.D. Candidate (and currently on the job market!) in Education Policy Analysis and Leadership at UC Riverside, working under the guidance of Dr. Cassandra Guarino. My dissertation uses machine learning to predict college readiness—an endeavor that sits at the intersection of education policy, big data, and advanced quantitative methods.

In my broader research, I explore three key areas: (1) using administrative data and predictive modeling to identify students at risk and guide early interventions, (2) evaluating how policy changes shape student outcomes, and (3) uncovering the relationships between diverse student characteristics and long-term success. Along the way, I’ve been fortunate to lead the quantitative analyses on multiple major research grants (NSF, IES, and the Haynes Foundation) and to receive support through the Dean’s Distinguished Fellowship.

Teaching is also a passion of mine. I’ve designed and instructed courses on education policy, research methods, and a graduate-level bootcamp in statistical programming—earning strong teaching evaluations and striving to create a welcoming space where students can voice their ideas, collaborate, and embrace hands-on learning. My biggest reward is seeing that spark of confidence when the pieces finally click into place—those “aha” moments that signal a genuine breakthrough.

I got my MS in Research, Evaluation, Measurement, and Statistics from UC Riverside in 2021, and a BS in Education and Public Policy (w/ Sociology minor) from Penn State in 2016. When I’m not crunching numbers, you’ll often find me out in nature, camera in hand, doing wildlife photography. My wife and I are also kept plenty busy by our two daughters, who are 5 and 2.
